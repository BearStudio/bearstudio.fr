We’re promised that AI will either free designers… or replace them. In real life? It produces generic screens, worthy of a free UI kit… and ugly. And to get that masterpiece, you have to write a prompt longer than The Odyssey. In other words, it’s closer to “your cousin who dabbles in design” than Apple’s design team at the keyboard.

Let’s be honest: my dream as a UX designer has never been to spend my days pushing pixels. What drives me is solving real problems for real users, with their very concrete constraints—turning ideas into apps that are pleasant, useful, and alive.

This talk is an unfiltered reflection after 15 years of experience in design and front-end development. So today, what does AI do well, and where does it spectacularly fail? And why, despite all the hype, designers still have a crucial role to play… at least until the next update.
