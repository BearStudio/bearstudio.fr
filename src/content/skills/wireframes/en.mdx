---
name: Wireframes
description: Low-fidelity visual blueprints defining page structure, content hierarchy, and user flows. Reduces development costs and aligns stakeholders early.
icon: mobile-wireframe
---

At BearStudio, wireframes are our first instinct when we need to design a new screen or feature that requires some thought. The idea is to lay out structure and interactions without visual noise getting in the way: no colors, no final typography, just the essentials.

It's a step that lets us validate a direction quickly before investing time in detailed design. We focus on what matters: how information is organized, user flows, interface logic. Feedback is easier to incorporate at this stage, because everyone is focused on substance rather than appearance.

Our wireframes are created in Figma, mainly by our designers or by developers with a knack for design. Once the structure is validated, we move on to the next step: prototyping.
